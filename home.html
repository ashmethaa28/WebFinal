South Korea

Introduction

 Officially known as the Republic of Korea, South Korea is a vibrant country located in East Asia. Having a population of over 51 million, South Korea is a highly developed nation rich in cultural heritage and has a strong economy. The country is located in the southern half of Korea and only shares one border with another nation, North Korea. All other edges are shared with the Yellow Sea to the west or the Sea of Japan to the east.

 South Korea's history dates back more than 5,000 years. Dynasties ruled a large portion of their history. The most well-known dynasty was the Joseon dynasty which led Korea to experience a gold age of culture and innovation. This dynasty developed some of the world's most valuable artistic and literary works.

 South Korea is now the world's 10th-largest economy due to its advanced technology. This country is the residence of some of the world's most well-known brands, such as Samsung, Kia, Hyundai, and LG. Due to these brands, South Korea is considered a leader in the technology and automotive industries.

 Pop culture within South Korea has been a roaring success. K-pop is a pop music genre that originated in South Korea and has massive fandoms worldwide supporting their favourite groups. In addition, the film industry is no short of being globalized and well-loved by the world. Some of the more famous media pieces created in South Korea are Parasite, Train to Busan, and Squid Games.

 This nation has made its mark on the world through its contributions. Its rich culture, technological advancements, and beautiful landscapes make it captivating globally and a destination everyone adds to their travel list.
